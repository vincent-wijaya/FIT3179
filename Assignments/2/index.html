About

Formula One is an international open-wheel single-seater formula racing which started back in 1950 which races all around the world. The word Formula refers to a set of regulations which all participating cars must follow. These regulations change season after season and together with reasearch and development, have led to the different-looking Formula One cars over the years.


Locations

Since 1967, Formula One have had at least 10 races each season and each race held at a different circuit in a different country. Presently, they have roughly 20 races a season.

Started off racing mostly in Europe, Formula One is now a spectacle that has been expanding its reach. To date, Formula One cars have raced on 79 different circuits in 34 different countries. The 2023 season will welcome a new circuit to the calendar as the teams will take on the Las Vegas Grand Prix for the first time.

Champions

In the current format, 20 drivers from 10 different constructors compete for the World Drivers' Champion and the World Constructors' Champion, whose inaugural season was the 1958 season. There have been 34 different World Drivers' Champion and 15 different World Constructors' Champion.

At the top of the World Drivers' Championship sits Michael Schumacher and Lewis Hamilton with 7 titles. With Lewis Hamilton entering the later years of his career, rises a new champion, a back-to-back champion in Max Verstappen, winning in 2021 and just recently, 2022.

Ferrari's domination in the 1970s and 2000s places them far in the lead with 16 titles. Mercedes spent 8 seasons at the top from 2014-2021, tying up McLaren's tally at 8 titles, but they'd need another period of supremacy to catch up to the "The Prancing Horse".

Seasonal Drivers' Standings

2014-2021 was a period which saw Lewis Hamilton around the top of the leaderboards, winning 81, races in a span of 8 seasons. He sits at the top in terms of race wins with 103 wins, 12 clear of the next highest, Michael Schumacher with 91.

Qualifying Time Progression

Formula 1 cars have been improving over the years. Despite the banning of ground effect since 1983, which reduced downforce, constructors have discovered other ways to go faster on the track. There is a conistent decreasing trend year after year depending on the circuit. Research and development on Formula 1 cars have also advanced technology of road cars.

One circuit which showed an obvious yearly decrease is the Australian Grand Prix in Melbourne. There were a couple of outliers in 2005 and 2013 due to damp track conditions which slowed down the cars massively.

Also in the British Grand Prix in Silverstone, there is downward trend of fastest qualifying time until a circuit layout change for the 2010 season. Since the change, there was again a downward slope, despite the few peaks due to rainy qualifying sessions in 2012, 2014, and 2017.

Another example is the Austria Grand Prix in Spielberg, there is a clear trend at least from 2014-2021 where cars are qualifying quicker each year, except for the rainy 2015 qualifying session.
